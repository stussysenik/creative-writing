# Columbia Reading Lisst Experimental Creative Writing Degree
note: generated by Perplexity.ai, possibly not entirely accurate

This curriculum replicates the breadth and depth of Columbia University's undergraduate and graduate creative writing programs, including the mandatory Core Curriculum in social sciences and humanities. It integrates foundational texts, experimental creative writing pedagogy, and interdisciplinary approaches to ensure a comprehensive education in experimental creativity.

---

## **Year 1: Foundations in Literature, Philosophy, and Writing**

### **Core Curriculum**
1. **Masterpieces of Western Literature and Philosophy (Literature Humanities)**  
   - Homer, *The Iliad*  
   - Sophocles, *Antigone*  
   - Plato, *The Republic*  
   - Dante, *The Divine Comedy*  
   - Shakespeare, *King Lear*  
   - Austen, *Pride and Prejudice*  
   - Dostoevsky, *Crime and Punishment*  

2. **University Writing**  
   - Gerald Graff and Cathy Birkenstein, *They Say/I Say: The Moves That Matter in Academic Writing*  

3. **Introduction to Contemporary Civilization in the West**  
   - Hobbes, *Leviathan*  
   - Locke, *Second Treatise of Government*  
   - Rousseau, *The Social Contract*  
   - Marx and Engels, *The Communist Manifesto*  

4. **Frontiers of Science** (Scientific reasoning seminar)  

---

### **Creative Writing Workshops**  
- **WRIT UN1100: Beginning Fiction Workshop**  
  - Raymond Carver, *Cathedral*  
  - Lorrie Moore, *Self-Help*  

- **WRIT UN1300: Beginning Poetry Workshop**  
  - Mary Oliver, *A Poetry Handbook*  

---

## **Year 2: Expanding Creative Horizons**

### **Core Curriculum Continuation**
1. **Masterpieces of Western Art**  
   - Gombrich, *The Story of Art*  

2. **Masterpieces of Western Music**  
   - Alex Ross, *The Rest Is Noise: Listening to the Twentieth Century*  

3. **Global Core Requirement** (Choose one)  
   - Chinua Achebe, *Things Fall Apart*  
   - Rabindranath Tagore, selected poems  

---

### **Creative Writing Seminars**
- **WRIT UN3121: How to Build a Person (Fiction Seminar)**  
  - Kazuo Ishiguro, *Never Let Me Go*  
  - Octavia Butler, *Kindred*

- **WRIT UN2311: Traditions in Poetry (Poetry Seminar)**  
  - T.S. Eliot, *The Waste Land and Other Poems*  
  - Claudia Rankine, *Citizen*

---

## **Year 3: Advanced Techniques and Interdisciplinary Exploration**

### **Core Curriculum**
1. **Social Science Electives** (Choose two)  
   - Michel Foucault, *Discipline and Punish*  
   - Benedict Anderson, *Imagined Communities*  

2. **Science/Quantitative Reasoning Elective** (e.g., Symbolic Logic or Foundations of Science)

---

### **Creative Writing Workshops**
- **WRIT UN3100: Advanced Fiction Workshop**  
  - Italo Calvino, *If on a Winter's Night a Traveler*  

- **WRIT UN3300: Advanced Poetry Workshop**  
  - John Ashbery, selected works  

---

### **Related Courses (Interdisciplinary)**
1. Philosophy: Maurice Merleau-Ponty, *Phenomenology of Perception*  
2. Anthropology: Clifford Geertz, *The Interpretation of Cultures*

---

## **Year 4: Capstone Projects and Cross-Genre Experimentation**

### **Senior Creative Writing Workshop**
- Students produce a significant body of work in their chosen genre under close faculty mentorship.

---

### **Cross-Genre Seminars**
- WRIT GU4011: Cross Genre Seminar – Imagining Berlin
  - W.G. Sebald, *Austerlitz*
  - Jenny Erpenbeck, *Visitation*

- WRIT UN3013: Process Writing & Writing Process
  - Anne Lamott, *Bird by Bird*
  - Natalie Goldberg, *Writing Down the Bones*

---

## **Graduate Years (MFA-Level Study)**

### Year 5: Deepening Craft
1. Advanced Craft Seminars:
   - WRIT GU4310: Documentary Poetics
     - Muriel Rukeyser, selected works
     - Layli Long Soldier, *Whereas*

2. Pedagogy:
   - Paulo Freire, *Pedagogy of the Oppressed*
   - bell hooks, *Teaching to Transgress*

3. Experimental Fiction:
   - Mark Z. Danielewski, *House of Leaves*
   - Eimear McBride, *A Girl Is a Half-Formed Thing*

---

### Year 6: Professionalization and Final Project
1. Literary Theory:
   - Roland Barthes, *S/Z*
   - Helene Cixous, "The Laugh of the Medusa"

2. Capstone Thesis:
   Students complete a full-length manuscript (novel/poetry collection/essay collection).

3. Community Engagement:
   Participation in Columbia Artist/Teachers (CA/T) or similar outreach programs.

---

This curriculum mirrors Columbia University's rigorous approach to creative writing while integrating its hallmark interdisciplinary Core Curriculum. It balances foundational texts with contemporary experimental works to cultivate both critical thinking and innovative literary practice.
